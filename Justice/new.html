Fundamental Rights

The Constitution of India guarantees fundamental rights to all citizens. These rights are essential for personal liberty and development.

Right to Equality (Articles 14-18)

All citizens are equal before the law and have equal protection of laws.

  • No discrimination based on religion, race, caste, sex, or place of birth
  • Equal opportunity in public employment
  • Abolition of untouchability

Right to Freedom (Articles 19-22)

Citizens have the freedom of speech, expression, assembly, association, movement, and profession.

  • Freedom of speech and expression
  • Freedom to assemble peacefully without arms
  • Freedom to form associations or unions
  • Freedom to move freely throughout India
  • Freedom to reside and settle in any part of India
  • Freedom to practice any profession or business

Right Against Exploitation (Articles 23-24)

Protection against exploitation of vulnerable sections of society.

  • Prohibition of traffic in human beings and forced labor
  • Prohibition of employment of children in factories, mines, or other hazardous employment

Right to Freedom of Religion (Articles 25-28)

Freedom to practice and propagate any religion.

  • Freedom to profess, practice, and propagate religion
  • Freedom to manage religious affairs
  • Freedom from religious instruction in certain educational institutions

Cultural and Educational Rights (Articles 29-30)

Protection of interests of minorities.

  • Protection of interests of minorities to preserve their language, script, and culture
  • Right of minorities to establish and administer educational institutions

Right to Constitutional Remedies (Article 32)

Right to move the Supreme Court for enforcement of Fundamental Rights.

  • File writs like Habeas Corpus, Mandamus, Prohibition, Quo Warranto, and Certiorari
  • Dr. B.R. Ambedkar called this "the heart and soul of the Constitution"

Privacy Rights

In 2017, the Supreme Court of India recognized the right to privacy as a fundamental right under Article 21 of the Constitution.

Data Protection Rights

Your personal data should be protected from misuse.

  • Right to control how your personal information is collected and used
  • Right to access data collected about you
  • Right to correct inaccurate information
  • Right to erasure of certain data

Digital Privacy

Protection against surveillance and monitoring.

  • Protection against unauthorized surveillance
  • Right to secure communications
  • Protection against phone tapping without proper authorization
  • Section 69 of the IT Act allows interception only under specific circumstances

Privacy in Public Spaces

Limits on surveillance and profiling in public areas.

  • Restrictions on CCTV monitoring without proper notice
  • Protection against unauthorized biometric data collection
  • Right to anonymity in certain contexts

Key Privacy Violations

Common violations and applicable sections:

  • Section 43A of IT Act: Compensation for failure to protect data
  • Section 66E: Punishment for violation of privacy
  • Section 67: Punishment for publishing obscene material in electronic form
  • Section 354C IPC: Voyeurism
  • Section 354D IPC: Stalking

Women's Rights

Indian women are protected by both constitutional guarantees and specific legislation designed to safeguard their rights and interests.

Protection Against Domestic Violence

The Protection of Women from Domestic Violence Act, 2005 provides protection against physical, sexual, verbal, emotional, and economic abuse.

  • Right to obtain protection orders
  • Right to residence in the shared household
  • Right to custody of children
  • Right to compensation and damages
  • Right to free legal aid

Sexual Harassment

The Sexual Harassment of Women at Workplace (Prevention, Prohibition and Redressal) Act, 2013 provides protection against workplace harassment.

  • Right to a safe working environment
  • Right to file complaints with the Internal Complaints Committee
  • Protection against retaliation
  • Right to request transfer of either party
  • Sections 354A, 354B, 354C, 354D of IPC deal with sexual harassment crimes

Maternity Benefits

The Maternity Benefit (Amendment) Act, 2017 provides various benefits.

  • 26 weeks of paid maternity leave
  • Work from home options after the leave period
  • Mandatory crèche facilities in establishments with 50+ employees
  • Protection against dismissal during pregnancy

Property Rights

Equal rights to property and inheritance.

  • Equal rights to parental property under the Hindu Succession (Amendment) Act, 2005
  • Right to property acquired during marriage
  • Rights to matrimonial property in case of divorce

Criminal Law Protections

Specific sections under the Indian Penal Code (IPC).

  • Section 376: Punishment for rape
  • Section 498A: Cruelty by husband or relatives
  • Section 304B: Dowry death
  • Section 366: Kidnapping or inducing a woman for marriage
  • Section 509: Insulting the modesty of a woman

Consumer Rights

The Consumer Protection Act, 2019 provides a comprehensive framework for protecting consumers against unfair trade practices.

Basic Consumer Rights

Fundamental rights every consumer has in India.

  • Right to safety
  • Right to information
  • Right to choose
  • Right to be heard
  • Right to seek redressal
  • Right to consumer education

Redressal Mechanisms

How to file complaints for consumer grievances.

  • District Consumer Disputes Redressal Commission: For claims up to ₹1 crore
  • State Consumer Disputes Redressal Commission: For claims between ₹1 crore and ₹10 crores
  • National Consumer Disputes Redressal Commission: For claims above ₹10 crores
  • Online filing through e-daakhil portal

E-commerce Protections

Special provisions for online shopping.

  • Right to return within defined period
  • Protection against misleading advertisements
  • Right to data privacy
  • Protection against unfair contract terms

Common Consumer Mistakes

Precautions to avoid problems.

  • Not checking product details carefully
  • Not keeping bills and receipts
  • Ignoring terms and conditions
  • Not reporting issues within the warranty period
  • Not understanding cancellation policies
